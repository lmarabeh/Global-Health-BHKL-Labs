Write-Up

Visualization Design Rationale

We selected a scatter plot as our primary visualization because it effectively captures the multidimensional relationship between economic prosperity (GDP per capita) and  health outcomes (life expectancy), while allowing additional variables to be encoded through color and size. Circle color represents the Aggregate Score, providing an integrated perspective of overall development, and circle size denotes population, emphasizing countries with broader demographic influence. The logarithmic x-axis was chosen after initially testing the raw GDP per capita values. Using the original scale resulted in an extremely uneven distribution, with high-income countries concentrated on the far right and most low and middle-income nations compressed near the origin. By applying a logarithmic transformation, we achieved a more balanced visual distribution that allows meaningful comparisons across all economic levels. In terms of interaction design, we incorporated a series of dynamic query techniques to promote intuitive exploration. The year slider enables users to examine temporal changes and development progress, while the region filter  facilitates comparisons among continents. Tooltips reveal country-specific details on demand, maintaining a clean and uncluttered interface. The addition of zoom functions further enhances usability, particularly when navigating densely populated regions of the plot. Together, these design choices create a cohesive and engaging visualization that invites users to investigate how economic and health indicators evolve and interact over time.

Development Process

At the checkpoint stage, our team explored multiple datasets and visualization concepts to identify a topic that would offer both analytical depth and visual clarity. After evaluating several possibilities, we agreed that global development and health indicators provided a meaningful and data-rich foundation for exploration. Each member created early prototypes to test feasibility, which led to the shared decision to build an interactive visualization highlighting the relationship between economic and health dimensions across countries. During the implementation phase, tasks were divided based on individual strengths and workflow priorities. Layth was responsible for data transformation and cleaning, calculating the Aggregate Score, and establishing the initial JavaScript framework, later refining the zoom interaction to improve usability. Khanh converted the static Python prototype into a D3 implementation, incorporating bubble visualization and responsive layout adjustments. Hangyi designed and implemented the year slider and legend, developed hover and highlighting interactions, and managed GitHub synchronization. Bee created the control panel layout, refined the year and region filters, and enhanced the zoom and interface design to ensure visual consistency and smooth user interaction. All members contributed to the final write-up, ensuring that the rationale and methodology were cohesive and accurately represented the collaborative process. Communication and version control were maintained through Discord and GitHub, allowing the team to iteratively test, review, and refine the visualization. Overall, the project was completed within approximately two weeks, requiring around 45–50 person-hours in total. Each member dedicated roughly 10–20 hours. Through consistent collaboration and iterative refinement, our team transformed a basic static chart into a comprehensive and exploratory visualization of global development patterns.
