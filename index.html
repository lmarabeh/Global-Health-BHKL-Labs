Overview

Welcome to our detailed analysis of global health trends and development indicators. This project provides a visualization of data from 188 countries over a ten year period (2012‚Äì2021), elucidating the intricate relationships among economic development, health outcomes, and quality of life.

At the core of our analysis lies the Aggregate Score: a composite index we developed to capture multidimensional development by integrating life expectancy, GDP per capita, access to safe water, immunization rates, and unemployment levels.

We developed this metric by selecting five indicators that represent distinct dimensions of development: life expectancy (health outcomes), GDP per capita (economic prosperity), access to safe water (infrastructure), immunization rates (public health capacity), and unemployment rates (economic opportunity). Each indicator was normalized to a 0‚Äì100 scale using min-max normalization, with unemployment reverse-scored prior to averaging to generate the final composite score.

Instead of considering these indicators individually, our composite measure demonstrates how various aspects of development interact with and influence each other. The interactive scatter plot presents GDP per capita on a logarithmic scale along the x-axis and life expectancy on the y-axis, with circle size indicating population and color signifying the Aggregate Score.

Users can analyze temporal patterns using a year slider, compare regions through filtering, and examine individual countries via tooltips. This design enables the identification of patterns, including the correlation between GDP and health, regional development trajectories, and countries that deviate from expected trends.
